# Privacy Policy

**Last Updated: March 31, 2026**

This Privacy Policy applies to the Minimal Piano application ("App").

## 1. Data Collection

The Minimal Piano App is designed to be a standalone, local application. 
- **We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal information or user data.**
- The App does not require you to create an account, log in, or provide any identifying details.
- The App does not track your usage patterns, keystrokes, or musical notes played.
- We use **LogRocket** solely for collecting essential debugging and performance insights (e.g., crash reports, session replays of errors). LogRocket does **not** collect any personally identifiable information (PII), and all data gathered is used exclusively for diagnosing and fixing bugs. No marketing, advertising, or user-profiling data is collected through this service.

## 2. Local Storage

The App may use your device's local storage solely to save your local preferences (such as your current octave or zoom level). This data remains entirely on your device and is never transmitted to us or any third parties.

## 2.1 Recording Hook

The App includes a recording feature (via the `useRecorder` hook) that allows you to capture your piano performances locally. All recorded audio data is stored entirely on your device and is **never** uploaded, transmitted, or shared with us or any third party. You have full control over your recordings, including the ability to export or delete them at any time.

## 3. Third-Party Services

This App is built using open-source frameworks (React Native and Expo). We integrate **LogRocket** exclusively for debugging purposes — it helps us identify and resolve crashes, errors, and performance issues. LogRocket may collect anonymised session-replay data (such as UI interactions that led to an error), but it does **not** collect personal data, advertising identifiers, or any data used for marketing. No other third-party analytics or advertising SDKs are integrated.

## 4. Children's Privacy

Our App is safe for children. Since we do not collect any personal data, we inadvertently comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and similar global regulations. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

## 5. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. Thus, you are advised to review this page periodically for any changes. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page. These changes are effective immediately after they are posted.
